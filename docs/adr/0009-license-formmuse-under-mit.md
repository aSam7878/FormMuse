# License FormMuse under MIT

The complete FormMuse V1 repository will be public under the standard MIT License. This includes the static website and its original layout implementation, documentation system, registry source and generation tooling, Form Templates, examples, tests, compatibility fixtures, CI configuration, and build scripts. MIT permits personal and commercial use, copying, modification, and redistribution while requiring preservation of the copyright and license notice in substantial copies. Adopters will not need visible FormMuse attribution in their interfaces.

The FormMuse name, logo, domain, and specifically designated Brand Assets remain outside the software licence. The standard root `LICENSE` stays unchanged. A root `TRADEMARKS.md` will govern name, logo, official-affiliation, and endorsement use; `public/brand/README.md` will identify the exact asset files excluded from MIT and their permitted uses. Both the root README and brand-assets README will state the software/brand boundary explicitly.

The policy will allow truthful, non-misleading references such as “built with FormMuse,” “compatible with FormMuse,” and “forked from FormMuse,” while prohibiting forks and derivatives from presenting themselves as official FormMuse or implying sponsorship, endorsement, or affiliation. It will not claim that the notice creates a trademark registration, claim an undefined visual style wholesale, or add restrictions to MIT-covered software. Qualified legal review in relevant jurisdictions is required before publishing the final policy. Secrets, deployment credentials, private security reports, and unpublished business material remain outside the public repository.

Public source does not change the Curated Library: V1 has no community template-submission workflow, public uploads, creator profiles, marketplace, or moderation system. MIT forks and modifications are allowed, but they do not enter the official FormMuse catalog. Magic UI is the transparency and code-ownership benchmark, not a source for copying its site layout, branding, prose, or assets.

Every Template Asset rendered inside a Template Preview iframe must be included in that template's distributed files; FormMuse will not use hidden preview-only artwork. Asset provenance and any separate licence obligations will remain in repository-side documentation and publication review. Meaningful images require useful alternative text, while purely decorative SVGs and icons must be hidden from assistive technology.
