# Separate FormMuse brand assets from MIT source

FormMuse-authored software and documentation will use the standard, unchanged MIT License. The FormMuse name, logo, domain, and only those Brand Asset files explicitly listed in `public/brand/README.md` remain outside MIT and are governed by the separate root `TRADEMARKS.md`. Both the root README and brand-assets README will use this boundary statement:

> FormMuse-authored software and documentation are licensed under the MIT License. The FormMuse name, logo, and brand assets identified in `public/brand/README.md` are not licensed under MIT and are subject to the FormMuse trademark and brand policy.

The brand policy will permit truthful, non-misleading statements including “built with FormMuse,” “compatible with FormMuse,” and “forked from FormMuse.” It will prohibit presenting a fork or derivative as official FormMuse and false claims of sponsorship, endorsement, or affiliation. It will not attempt to reserve an undefined visual style, assert ownership of the word in unrelated contexts, treat the notice as trademark registration, or restrict software rights granted by MIT.

Brand files remain publicly inspectable but are a clearly identified licence exception. Their permitted display and reference uses must be stated instead of relying only on a blanket “all rights reserved” sentence. Qualified legal review in each relevant jurisdiction is a public-launch requirement for the final policy wording.
